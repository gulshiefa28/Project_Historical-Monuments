East zone

The East Zone is known for its rich history and vibrant culture. It encompasses regions that have been central to trade, education, and traditional crafts. Visitors can explore ancient landmarks, bustling markets, and scenic landscapes that showcase the heritage and natural beauty of the area. From historic monuments to modern urban centers, the East Zone offers a unique blend of the past and present.

Angkor Wat (Cambodia)

Angkor Wat is the largest religious monument in the world, built by the Khmer Empire in the 12th century. It is famous for its detailed stone carvings and beautiful temple towers. The site represents the rich cultural and architectural history of ancient Cambodia.

Forbidden City (China)

The Forbidden City in Beijing served as the imperial palace for Chinese emperors for almost 500 years. It is known for its massive size, red walls, and golden roofs. The complex reflects the rich traditions and craftsmanship of ancient Chinese dynasties.

Borobudur Temple (Indonesia)

Borobudur is a 9th-century Buddhist temple built in the style of a giant pyramid. It contains thousands of stone carvings and Buddha statues. The monument shows the spiritual and artistic achievements of early Indonesian civilization.
